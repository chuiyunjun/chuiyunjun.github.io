1. Intorduction


The project aims to design and build a ML-based analytics platform for CPPIB (Canada Pension Plan Investment Board) building predictive maintenance given building data shared by our client, Modern Niagara group (MNG), Canada's largest mechanical contractor



2. What Is Predictive Maintenance




MNG currently takes a manual approach to building maintenance, and repairs are done in accordance to tenant complaints as well as scheduled check-ups. However, this can lead to costly delays caused by repairs scheduled at inconvenient times. Our team's proposed solution is to generate automated alerts for when there is a potential future fault, so that issues can be prevented rather than reactively fixed. The proposed Digital Twin is data-driven representation of the VAV (Variable Air Volume, i.e. each room of CPPIB building) and Central handling Unit's operational behaviour, and a ML-based analytics platform for CPPIB building maintenance. After the digital twin generates predictions of how future system parameters are intended to behave, the predictions will be fed into a ML-driven anomaly detector to flag outlier points, and to predict faults ahead of time.
