1. Intorduction


First, to recap how score-based diffusion models work. On the forward evolution of the SDE(stochastic differential equation), we solve an SDE from t=0 to t=T (using diffrax solvers). At t=0 we have our perfect uncorrupted training example, from our dataset. At t=T we have added so much noise that we have something that looks like a random Gaussian vector. And now for all possible t in this interval, we train our score-based model to try and determine how to slightly denoise a sample. Then, sampling a random Gaussian vector and repeatedly denoising (i.e. solving the reverse ODE/SDE) is our inference-time procedure for producing a sample image





That inference-time procedure for producing sample is called 'denoising' now what I notice is that objective, called the score-matching function is VERY sensitive in what is picked for λ

what is currently happening is that given an SDE that diffuses data at t=0 to a normal at t=1: constant λ will essentially fail to train, while a λ that upweights the t=1 end of things will. This is really surprising to me. I would have thought that the upweighting the t=0 (i.e. clean data) end of things would be more important, as during inference that where we put the "finishing touches" on denoising our sample. so the main goal of this project is to address: why do we get poorer-quality samples when we've explicitly changed our loss function so that the network should do a better job? (e.g. upweighting near t=0)
